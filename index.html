FICO Score (Credit Score)

A three-digit number lenders use to determine how likely you are to repay a loan.

Fair Isaac Corporation. Landlords use it, and sometimes employers, Insurance companies, etc. FICO Scores are used by 90% of the top lenders.

What affects your credit score?

  • 35% - Payment History

  • 10% - Credit Mix

Open a Retirement Account

How to get a Retirement Account

  • Through your Employer
  • Set one up for your business
  • Open one up on your own
There are more than these, but these are the ones I'm covering.

Kinds of Retirement Accounts

  • 401K / 403B
  • IRAs (Individual Retirement Account)
  • SEP Plans (Simplified Employee Pension)

401K / 403B

  • For bigger businesses or Non-profit organizations
  • Money goes in pre-taxed
  • Your employer can contribute in addition to what you contribute
  • Your employer picks funds for you or allows you to pick from a list
  • Contribution limit is $13,500 in 2021 and 2020 ($13,000 in 2019)
  • When you withdraw $ in retirement it's taxed as regular income

Simple IRA

  • For small businesses
  • Money goes in pre-taxed
  • Your employer can contribute in addition to what you contribute
  • You control your investments
  • Contribution limit is $13,500 in 2021 and 2020 ($13,000 in 2019)
  • When you withdraw $ in retirement it's taxed as regular income

SEP Plans (Simplified Employee Pension)

  • For Solopreneurs or small businesses with fluctuating cash flow
  • Your employer contributes
  • Contribution limit is 25% of the employee's compensation, or $57,000 for 2020 and $58,000 for 2021 ($56,000 for 2019)

Traditional IRA

  • For working individuals
  • Open it yourself (in addition to one provided by your employer)
  • Money going in is tax-deductible
  • You control your investments
  • Contribution limit is $6,000 ($7,000 if you're 50+)
  • When you withdraw $ in retirement it's taxed as regular income

ROTH IRA üéâ

  • For working individuals
  • Open it yourself (in addition to one provided by your employer)
  • Money going in is after taxes
  • You control your investments
  • Contribution limit is $6,000 ($7,000 if you're 50+)
  • You must make < $198,000 as a married couple and < $125,000 as a single person
  • When you withdraw $ in retirement it's TAX FREE

Where can I open an IRA?

ETFs (Exchange Traded Funds)

An exchange traded fund (ETF) is a type of investment that tracks a stock market index and can be purchased or sold on a stock exchange the same way a regular stock can.

It's like investing in a sample or the entire stock market itself.

Stock Market Indexes

  • Dow Jones Industrial Average ("The Dow") - An index of 30 prominent companies.
  • S&P 500 - An index tracking the performance of 500 large companies
  • NASDAQ Composite Index - Tracks the entire NASDAQ exchange.
  • And more!

Stock Splits

A stock split increases the number of shares in a company.

If you have 10 shares of a stock at $100 each

  • Before split - 10 shares at $100 each = $1,000
  • After a 2-for-1 split - 20 shares at $50 each = $1,000
You have more stocks at a lower price but it will grow.

Stock Splits

If you bought 10 shares of Apple stock in 1986

  • June 1987 2-for-1 = 20 shares
  • June 2000 2-for-1 = 40 shares
  • February 2005 2-for-1 = 80 shares
  • June 2014 7-for-1 = 560 shares
  • August 2020 4-for-1 = 2,240 shares

Dollar Cost Averaging

Dollar-cost averaging is an investing approach involving investing a set amount of money on a consistent schedule regardless of the market price.

Dollar Cost Averaging

Set it and forget it. You can automate mutual fund investing.

You want the market to go down.

Buy low sell high (and use dollar cost averaging).

When you log in and see your account took a dip, don't freak out. See it as an opportunity.
